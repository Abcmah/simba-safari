Witness the famed wildebeest migration from Masai Mara to Ngorongoro, offering the ultimate wildlife experience across the plains of East Africa.

All about the Trip
Best Season: Dry season Late July - October
Rainy Season: November - March, May - Early July

Dry Season: During this period, the weather is mild and dry, making wildlife viewing easier as animals congregate around water sources. Additionally, the dry season coincides with the Great Migration in the Masai Mara and Serengeti, offering the opportunity to witness this extraordinary natural spectacle.

Embark on an immersive journey from Nairobi, Kenya, delving into the rich tapestry of East Africa's wilderness. Your adventure commences at Lake Nakuru, famed for its kaleidoscopic flocks of flamingos and diverse birdlife. Amidst the verdant landscapes, keep your eyes peeled for rhinos, giraffes, and other wildlife that call this picturesque park home.

From Lake Nakuru, traverse to the legendary Masai Mara, where the expansive plains play host to the iconic Big Five and an abundance of wildlife. Set out on exhilarating game drives, where encounters with lions, elephants, buffaloes, and leopards await, offering unforgettable moments in the heart of the African savannah. Continuing your expedition into Tanzania, discover the sweeping vistas of the Serengeti National Park, renowned for its dramatic landscapes and the mesmerizing spectacle of the annual wildebeest migration. Your journey also includes a visit to the Ngorongoro Crater, a natural amphitheater teeming with diverse wildlife, providing an immersive safari experience like no other.

    1. Day 1 Nairobi To Lake Nakuru

      Your adventure begins with a hotel or airport pick-up in Nairobi in the morning, setting the stage for an unforgettable journey. Departing from Nairobi, you'll embark on a scenic drive through the Great Rift Valley, soaking in breathtaking landscapes along the way.

      Arrive at Lake Nakuru National Park in time for a sumptuous lunch, fueling up for the exciting exploration ahead. Spend the afternoon on a thrilling game drive around Lake Nakuru, where you'll have the chance to spot flamingos, rhinos, giraffes, and an array of other wildlife species.

      As the day winds down, retreat to a lodge or camp near Lake Nakuru for a relaxing dinner and an overnight stay, immersing yourself in the tranquility of the African wilderness.

    2. Day 2 L.Nakuru - Masai Mara

      After breakfast at Lake Nakuru, you'll travel to the renowned Masai Mara National Reserve, arriving at your lodge or camp in time for a delicious lunch. In the afternoon, embark on an adrenaline game drive across the vast savannahs of Masai Mara, where you will have the opportunity to see the Big Five and a variety of other wildlife species. As the sun sets, return to your lodging for a tasty dinner and a good night's sleep.

      Masai Mara National Reserve is well-known for its amazing animal sightings, which include iconic species such as lions, leopards, and elephants. Birdwatchers will be impressed by the reserve's nearly 500 bird species. The reserve's various landscapes, which include wide savannahs and acacia trees, provide an ideal setting for outstanding safari adventures. Witnessing the historic Great Wildebeest Migration, which takes place between July and October, offers a new and unforgettable dimension to your safari experience.

    3. Day 3 full day masai mara

      Embark on a full-day game drive in Masai Mara, experiencing the awe-inspiring wildebeest migration during the seasonal period from July to September. You can also opt to visit a Masai village for a glimpse into local culture and traditions. Enjoy a picnic lunch in the park and continue with afternoon game viewing. Return to your lodge or camp for dinner and an overnight stay.

      Alternatively, explore the vast plains of Masai Mara on a full-day game drive, encountering lions, elephants, rhinos, and more. Enhance your adventure with a balloon safari offering a unique aerial perspective of the savannah, followed by a champagne bush breakfast. Lunch will be provided as picnic lunch boxes amidst the wildlife, with game viewing continuing until your return to camp in the evening.

    4. Day 4 Mara To serengeti

      Following an early morning breakfast and packing picnic lunch boxes, set out on a scenic drive towards the Kenya-Tanzania border at Isebania. Prepare to obtain your Tanzanian visa, ensuring a seamless transition into the Serengeti. Upon clearing immigration formalities, continue your journey with your experienced driver/guide into the endless plains of the Serengeti. Enjoy your first game drive en route to the camp, marveling at the diverse wildlife that inhabits this iconic national park.

      Arrive at your camp in the late afternoon, where a warm welcome and safety briefing await from the camp manager. Prepare for an authentic African safari experience as you settle in for the night amidst the untamed beauty of the Serengeti. Don't forget to budget for your Tanzanian visa fee, ranging from $50 to $100, depending on your nationality.

    5. Day 5 Full Day Serengeti

      Explore the Serengeti National Park with a full-day safari tailored to your preferences. Options include morning and afternoon game drives or a thrilling full-day excursion with a picnic lunch. Your guide will lead you to areas where wildlife herds are most active for unforgettable sightings. Don't miss the chance to visit the Serengeti Visitor Centre to learn about park history and conservation efforts. End the day with dinner at your lodge and a peaceful night's rest under the stars. Consider adding an early hot air balloon ride for a unique perspective.

    6. Day 6 serengeti To Ngorongoro

      Start your day with an early breakfast at 6:00 am, then head out for one last game drive through the Serengeti before leaving the park. Next, travel to Ngorongoro with picnic lunches packed for the journey. Descend into the crater floor for a half-day exploration, where you'll encounter an array of wildlife in this exceptional ecosystem. Traverse the two roads linking the rim and floor, marveling at the diverse landscapes and abundant wildlife within the crater. After your crater adventure, return to your lodge for a delightful dinner and overnight stay.

    7. Day 7 Ngorongoro To Tarangire

      Embark on an exhilarating full-day game drive in the Ngorongoro Crater, Africa's wildlife haven, boasting the continent's densest concentration of wildlife. Witness lions, elephants, buffalo, rhinos, and leopards roaming freely amidst stunning scenery. After a refreshing picnic lunch, journey to Tarangire National Park for an evening game drive. Keep your eyes peeled for elephants, baobab trees, and diverse wildlife before returning to your lodge or camp for a delicious dinner and a restful night amidst the sounds of the African bush.

    8. Day 8 Tarangire To Amboseli

      Depart Tarangire after breakfast for the border, then continue to Amboseli National Park in Kenya. Enjoy lunch upon arrival before embarking on an afternoon game drive, where you'll have the chance to witness Amboseli's famous elephant herds and stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro. Return to your lodge or camp for dinner and an overnight stay, surrounded by the natural beauty of Amboseli.

    9. Day 9 Amboseli To Nairobi

      Start your day with an early morning game drive in Amboseli, capturing the breathtaking sunrise and iconic photographs of elephants against the backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro. Afterward, return to your accommodation for a hearty breakfast before bidding farewell to Amboseli and embarking on your journey back to Nairobi. As you travel, reflect on the unforgettable wildlife encounters and majestic scenery you've experienced. Arrive in Nairobi in the afternoon, where you'll either be dropped off at your hotel to relax or transferred to the airport for your departure, concluding your memorable safari adventure.

    1. What is the best time to visit Lake Nakuru?

    - The best time to visit Lake Nakuru is during the dry seasons from January to March and July to October for optimal wildlife viewing and birdwatching opportunities.

    2. What wildlife can I expect to see at Lake Nakuru?

    - Lake Nakuru is famous for its flamingos, but you can also spot other wildlife such as rhinos, lions, giraffes, zebras, and various bird species.

    3. Is it safe to visit Lake Nakuru National Park?

    - Yes, Lake Nakuru National Park is generally safe for visitors. However, it's essential to follow the instructions of your guide and adhere to park regulations.

    4. Are there accommodation options within Lake Nakuru National Park?

    - Yes, there are several lodges and camps within and near the park, offering various accommodation options ranging from luxury lodges to budget-friendly campsites.

    5. What activities can I do besides game drives at Lake Nakuru?

    - Besides game drives, visitors can enjoy birdwatching, nature walks, picnics, and boat safaris (when water levels permit) at Lake Nakuru.

    6. What should I pack for the safari?

    -It's recommended to pack sunscreen, insect repellent, a hat, sunglasses, comfortable walking shoes, and lightweight clothing. Don't forget your camera and binoculars for wildlife viewing!

    7. Are there opportunities for photography during the safari?

    - Yes, plenty of chances to capture wildlife sightings, landscapes, and memorable moments.

    8.Is there an entrance fee to Lake Nakuru National Park?

    -Yes, there is an entrance fee to Lake Nakuru National Park for both residents and non-residents, which varies depending on nationality and vehicle type.
