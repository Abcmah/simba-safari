All about Masai Mara, Lake Nakuru,Hells Gate
Best Season: January - February, June - October

Wet Season: March - May, November - December

Suitable for bird watching as migratory birds flock the region

Best Season: With animals gathering around water sources, spotting them becomes effortless. This timing also aligns perfectly with the renowned Mara migration in Masai Mara.

Masai Mara: Delve into the heart of the African wilderness as you traverse the expansive plains of Masai Mara. Witness the majestic Big Five: lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinos—in their natural habitat. Be captivated by the annual wildebeest migration, a spectacle unlike any other, and soak in the rich culture of the Maasai people.

Lake Nakuru And Naivasha: Discover the allure of Kenya's freshwater wonders at Lake Nakuru and Lake Naivasha. At Lake Nakuru, be mesmerized by the sight of flamingos adorning the lake's surface in a pink spectacle. As you navigate the tranquil waters of Lake Naivasha, encounter a diverse array of birdlife, including majestic fish eagles and vibrant kingfishers. Amidst lush vegetation and serene landscapes, both lakes offer opportunities for peaceful boat safaris, nature walks, and moments of pure relaxation amidst nature's splendor.

Hell's Gate: Step into a realm of geological wonders at Hell's Gate National Park, characterized by towering cliffs, rugged gorges, and steaming geothermal vents. Embark on thrilling hikes or bike rides through the breathtaking landscape, and immerse yourself in the park's rich biodiversity, which includes diverse wildlife such as zebras, giraffes, and baboons.

    1. Day 1 Nairobi To Hells Gate

      Embark on an adventure from Nairobi to Hell's Gate National Park, celebrated for its dramatic cliffs and ancient volcanic formations. Explore on foot or by bike, encountering diverse wildlife like zebras and giraffes.

      Thrill-seekers can indulge in rock climbing, an optional activity available at an additional cost. After the exhilarating climb, continue your journey to Lake Nakuru to marvel at its pink-hued flamingos and abundant birdlife, completing your unforgettable day of exploration. Rest for the night at City Max, preparing for more adventures to come.

    2. Day 2 Lake Nakuru & Naivasha

      Following breakfast, you'll set off for a captivating game drive at Lake Nakuru, immersing yourself in the park's wonders for approximately four hours.

      Afterwards, we'll depart and journey towards Lake Naivasha, where a delectable lunch awaits. Following your meal, you'll have the option to partake in a walking safari or a scenic boat ride, with payments made directly.

      Conclude the day with a delightful dinner and overnight stay at the lodge.

    3. Day 3 Masai Mara Transfer

      After your exploration of Lake Nakuru or Naivasha, continue your journey to the iconic Masai Mara, where you'll spend the night at Mara Sopa Lodge.

      Nestled in the heart of the wilderness, the lodge offers unparalleled views of the savannah. Relax in comfort in well-appointed rooms and savor delicious meals at the lodge's restaurant.

      Gather around the campfire under the starry sky for an unforgettable evening, filled with stories and laughter. Rest peacefully, ready for an exciting day of wildlife sightings in the Mara.

    4. Day 4 Full Day Masai Mara

      Enjoy an early breakfast before setting out for a full day of game driving with a packed lunch. Explore the Masai Mara Reserve, with excellent chances to spot the Big Five and other wildlife. Have a picnic lunch by the Mara River. Return to camp in the late afternoon for dinner and overnight stay.

    5. Day 5 Masai Mara To Nairobi

      Check out in the morning after breakfast and head out to a game drive on your way out. Along the way, enjoy a stop at the Great Rift Valley viewpoint before continuing to Nairobi.

      On arrival you will be dropped off at te airport for your flight or hotel of your picking(neither is covered in cost)

    • What are the highlights of the safari?
    • Frequenty Asked Questions

    Masai Mara: Explore the iconic Masai Mara National Reserve, known for its abundant wildlife, including the Big Five (lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinos). Witness the breathtaking spectacle of the annual wildebeest migration (seasonal), and immerse yourself in the rich culture of the Maasai people.

    Lake Nakuru: Marvel at the stunning scenery of Lake Nakuru, famous for its vast flocks of flamingos that create a mesmerizing pink hue against the lake's surface. Encounter diverse birdlife and perhaps spot endangered rhinos in the nearby sanctuary.

    Lake Naivasha: Experience the tranquility of Lake Naivasha, a freshwater oasis surrounded by lush vegetation. Enjoy boat safaris to observe hippos and birdlife, and take leisurely walks along the lake's shores while enjoying stunning views of Mount Longonot.

    Hells Gate National Park: Discover the unique landscapes of Hells Gate National Park, characterized by towering cliffs, deep gorges, and geothermal activity. Engage in thrilling activities such as hiking, rock climbing, and cycling amidst the park's breathtaking scenery.

    Cultural Experiences: Immerse yourself in the local culture with optional visits to Maasai villages or cultural centers, where you can learn about traditional customs, dances, and crafts.

    Here's a summarized version of the FAQ with the main questions numbered: 1. What is the best time to visit these destinations?

    - The Masai Mara is excellent year-round, with the wildebeest migration typically occurring from July to October. Lake Nakuru and Naivasha are best visited during the dry season from June to October and January to February to see more wildlife.

    2. Are meals included in the package?

    - Yes, most packages include meals such as breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Some packages may offer packed lunches during game drives or excursions.

    3. What types of accommodations are included?

    - Accommodations range from lodges to tented camps, offering comfortable stays in the wilderness.

    4. What should I pack for the trip?

    - It's recommended to pack comfortable clothing suitable for safari outings, including lightweight and breathable fabrics, closed-toe shoes for walking safaris, a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, and binoculars for wildlife viewing.

    5. Is travel insurance necessary for the trip?

    - Travel insurance is highly recommended to cover any unforeseen circumstances such as medical emergencies, trip cancellations, or lost luggage during your safari adventure.

    6. What should I pack for the safari?

    - Recommended items include lightweight clothing, a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes, binoculars, and a camera with extra batteries and memory cards.

    7. Are there opportunities for photography during the safari?

    - Yes, plenty of chances to capture wildlife sightings, landscapes, and memorable moments.

    8. How physically demanding is the safari?

    - Most activities are suitable for individuals of varying fitness levels, but some walking may be involved. It's essential to inform your tour operator of any mobility concerns.

    9. Can I customize the itinerary to suit my preferences?

    - Yes we offer customizable itineraries, allowing you to tailor the trip according to your interests, budget, and schedule. Be sure to inquire about any customization options available.
