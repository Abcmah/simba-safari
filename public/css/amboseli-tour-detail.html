All about Amboselli

Amboseli National Park, covering approximately 392 square kilometers, is situated in southern Kenya near the Tanzanian border. Renowned for its iconic views of Mount Kilimanjaro, the park's diverse ecosystems include savannahs, swamps, and woodlands.

Conservation Efforts:

Amboseli is actively involved in conservation initiatives, particularly in the protection of elephants and their habitats. Research and monitoring programs contribute to the understanding and preservation of the park's unique ecosystem.

With its breathtaking scenery and rich biodiversity, Amboseli National Park stands as a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic African safari experience.

    1. Day 1 Day 1 Amboseli

      This itinerary offers a captivating journey from Nairobi to Amboseli National Park, promising a delightful mix of relaxation and wildlife exploration. Departing at 0730hrs from Hotel or airport, the scenic drive along the Nairobi-Mombasa Highway provides a picturesque backdrop for the 3-4 hour journey to Amboseli. Upon arrival, guests can savor a well-timed lunch before settling into their chosen lodge or camp, immersing themselves in the tranquility of the surroundings.

      The afternoon adventure unfolds with an exhilarating game drive, set against the stunning backdrop of Mt. Kilimanjaro. Witnessing majestic elephants, many adorned with impressive ivory tusks, adds a touch of awe to the raw beauty of nature. The safari extends to big game viewing and bird watching, ensuring an immersive experience in the heart of Amboseli's wildlife habitat. As the day concludes, the tour guide escorts guests back to their reserved accommodation for a relaxing dinner and a restful overnight stay, setting the stage for more adventures in the days to come.

    2. Day 2 fullday Amboseli

      Embrace early mornings on safari for an adventurous start. After breakfast, get picked up from your lodge for a thrilling day in Amboseli. Marvel at Mount Kilimanjaro's views before clouds gather. Spot lions, leopards, and cheetahs as they roam the savannah.

      Our game drive includes Observation Hill for panoramic park views. Watch herds of elephants up close, discover 80+ mammals, and 400+ bird species. Enhance your safari with a visit to a Masai village, experiencing their daily life, dances, and rituals. Enjoy a picnic lunch and return to your lodge in the evening for relaxation, dinner, and an overnight stay.

    3. Day 3 Amboseli - Nairobi

      Wake up early for your final Amboseli adventure. Enjoy hotel amenities, a swim, and a last game run after breakfast. Check out and join the guide for a rewarding drive out, spotting elephants, buffalos, and lions. Witness predator activity and the stunning Mount Kilimanjaro.

      After, take a relaxed drive via Nairobi-Mombasa Highway, arriving in the city by lunch. We'll drop you off at Nairobi CBD or your requested point, concluding the tour.

    Game Drives: Morning and afternoon game drives offer opportunities to witness the park's diverse wildlife, with a chance to see predators in action during the cooler parts of the day.

    Observation Hill: This vantage point provides stunning panoramic views of the park, Mount Kilimanjaro, and the surrounding landscapes, making it an ideal spot for photography.

    Cultural Experiences: Interaction with the Maasai people adds a cultural dimension to the Amboseli experience. Visitors can learn about their traditions, enjoy traditional dances, and even visit a Maasai village.

    Which animals are we to see

    Elephants: Amboseli is known for its vast herds of elephants, who are frequently seen against the backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro. The elephants here are famed for their massive tusks.

    Predators: Lions, leopards, and cheetahs are among the predators that roam the park. The wide-open plains make it easier to spot these majestic big cats in action.

    buffalo: Amboseli is home to large buffalo herds, which may often be seen grazing throughout the park.

    Birdlife: The park is a birdwatcher's paradise, with over 400 species documented. The rich birdlife includes flamingos, pelicans, kingfishers, and raptors.
