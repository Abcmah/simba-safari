All about Mount Kenya
Best Season: Sunny Season Jnauar - March, June - October
Popular Location: Madrid, Bhamas, Phuket, Sydney

Mount Kenya, Africa's second-highest peak after Kilimanjaro, is a remarkable sight. This extinct volcano, located in Kenya, stands as the nation's highest mountain.

Despite its location near the equator, Mount Kenya is crowned with snow, a rare phenomenon. The mountain boasts a series of glaciers, including Northey, Kraft, Darwin, and Diamond, with Lewis being the largest. Sadly, these glaciers are rapidly melting due to climate change and may disappear entirely within the next 30 years.

Mount Kenya offers a range of ecosystems, from lush forests to alpine meadows, making it a haven for biodiversity. The mountain is also culturally significant, with local communities, such as the Kikuyu and Meru, considering it sacred and hosting various myths and traditions associated with it. Mount Kenya National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, offers opportunities for trekking, wildlife viewing, and adventure sports, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

  • Tips
    1. Day 1 Nairobi To Old Moses Mount Camp

      Embark on a road trip to Sirimon gate and enjoy a picnic lunch before entry. The hike begins from the gate (2660m), ascending through magnificent mountain forest, bamboo, and giant heather zones before reaching the high-altitude moorland and our first hut, Old Moses camp (3,300m), where you'll enjoy evening tea, mountain entertainment, dinner, and overnight stay.

      This gentle trek takes 3-4 hours, gaining an elevation of 680m, offering stunning views of Batian and Nelion peaks. After being picked up from the airport or a Nairobi hotel, you'll head to Nanyuki town for lunch, crossing the equator en route to Sirimon Park gate. Dinner and overnight accommodation await at Old Moses Camp.

    2. Day 2 Old Moses To Shipton camp

      After a healthy breakfast, take a lovely hike to Shipton's Camp (4200m), traveling 17 kilometers in 6 to 8 hours through Likii Valley in the morning. Along the way, take a water break and continue on the track, crossing the Ontulili River before contouring into the Moorland and crossing the Likii North River and Mackinders Valley, which provide stunning scenery and photo possibilities.

      Before arriving to Shipton's Camp, take in panoramic views of the peaks from the lookout and eat a nutritious lunch by the Likii River. Enjoy evening tea, snacks, dinner, and an overnight stay in this surreal location beneath towering peaks and glaciers, surrounded by a resident population of rock hyrax and sunbirds.

    3. Day 3 Shipton rest Day

      Consider a rest day at Shipton Camp to aid acclimatization. You can take short walks or day excursions to Oblong and Hausberg tarns, ascending 370m before returning to camp for lunch. Enjoy some free time in the afternoon, possibly lazing in the sun while watching the eagles.

      Remember that ascending high and sleeping low helps with acclimatization, but you can get the same impact by enjoying the sunshine and eagle sightings. Shipton Camp serves dinner and provides an overnight stay.

    4. Day 4 Shipton To Mackinders via Kami Camp

      From Shipton's Camp, the trail leads to Kami Camp, which is located beneath the north face of Batian. The path continues west-southwest, ascending over switchbacks in the snow to Hausberg Col at 4590m.

      As we descend into the upper Hausberg valley, we stop at the picturesque Oblong and Hausberg tarns. As we climb to the peak of Arthur's Seat ridge, we notice the trail snaking around beautiful rock ledges. Finally, we descend to Mackinder's Camp, where we will stay the night.

    5. Day 5 Mackinders To Lenana Point

      Begin your journey at 2:30 a.m., aiming to reach Point Lenana for sunrise in a 4-hour trek, with Kilimanjaro visible on the horizon. After that, head to Mintos Hut for a substantial breakfast. After a quick rest, travel down to Mt Kenya Bandas via the Gorges Valley for supper and overnight accommodation.

      Meru Bandas offers the pleasures of wood fireplaces, hot showers, and cozy mattresses.

    6. Day 6 Bandas To Nairobi

      Following your final breakfast, descend over a broad track through bamboo and pristine rainforest, where you will meet your car for the return trip to Nairobi via Chogoria town, arriving in the mid to late afternoon.

    1. Best Time to Visit Mount Kenya:

    - Optimal Months: Consider trekking during the dry seasons (January to February and July to October) for favorable weather conditions and clear skies.

    - Peak Climbing Season: Plan your visit during the peak climbing season for bustling trails and vibrant wildlife sightings.

    - Off-Peak Benefits: Explore Mount Kenya during the off-peak seasons (March to June and November to December) for quieter trails and potentially lower accommodation rates.

    2. Clothing and Gear:

    Specific recommendations on clothing, footwear, and equipment essential for the trek, including layers for varying temperatures and sturdy hiking boots.

    3. Altitude-Related Concerns: :

    -Learn about altitude sickness symptoms and preventive measures like gradual ascent and staying hydrated.

    -Understand the importance of acclimatization and recognize severe symptoms that may require descent.

    -Stay informed about altitude sickness medications and adopt a slow and steady approach to trekking at high elevations.

    4. Difficulty of Trekking Routes:

    -Trekking routes on Mount Kenya vary in difficulty, from moderate to challenging, depending on factors like elevation gain and terrain ruggedness.

    -Popular routes like the Sirimon, Chogoria, and Naro Moru offer diverse landscapes and varying levels of difficulty, suitable for different trekking preferences and fitness levels.

    -It's important to choose a route that matches your fitness level and trekking experience, and consider guided tours for assistance in navigating challenging terrain and ensuring safety.

    5. Age and Fitness Requirements:

    -While there are typically no strict age limits for Mount Kenya treks, a good level of physical fitness is essential due to the challenging terrain and high altitudes.

    -Trekking Mount Kenya requires stamina, endurance, and strength, with preparation recommended through regular cardiovascular exercise and strength training.

    -It's important to assess your fitness level honestly and choose a trekking route that matches your abilities, considering guided tours for support and safety if necessary.

    6. Wildlife Encounters:

    -Mount Kenya's diverse ecosystem supports a variety of wildlife, including mammals like elephants, buffaloes, and various antelope species, along with smaller mammals like monkeys and rodents.

    -Birdwatchers can spot numerous bird species, including raptors like eagles and hawks, as well as colorful songbirds and waterfowl in the alpine and forested regions.

    -While encounters with larger predators like lions and leopards are rare due to the dense vegetation, the chance to observe and photograph unique wildlife in their natural habitat adds to the allure of trekking in Mount Kenya National Park.
