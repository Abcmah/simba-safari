All about Mount Kenya, Masai Mara
Best Season: Sunny Season Jnauar - March, June - October
Popular Location: Madrid, Bhamas, Phuket, Sydney

Embark on a remarkable 5-day expedition through Kenya's most iconic destinations, from the majestic slopes of Mount Kenya to the vast plains of the Masai Mara. Your adventure begins with awe-inspiring views of Mount Kenya at Amboseli National Park, followed by an exclusive wildlife encounter in Ol Pejeta Conservancy, home to endangered species such as rhinos and chimpanzees.

Continue your journey to the legendary Masai Mara National Reserve, where you'll witness the spectacle of the Great Migration and encounter the Big Five in their natural habitat. Immerse yourself in the rich Maasai culture with visits to local villages and enjoy thrilling game drives across the savannah plains.

After unforgettable wildlife encounters and breathtaking landscapes, conclude your safari with a return to Nairobi, where you'll depart with cherished memories of Kenya's diverse wildlife and stunning natural beauty.

  • Tips
    1. Day 1 Nairobi To Mt-Kenya

      Embark on a seamless transfer from Nairobi to the enchanting slopes of Mount Kenya.

      Departing from Nairobi, your journey will take you through scenic landscapes and charming countryside villages as you make your way towards the majestic mountain. Along the route, you'll have the opportunity to marvel at breathtaking vistas and immerse yourself in the tranquility of Kenya's rural scenery. Whether you choose to travel by road or opt for a scenic flight, your transfer to Mount Kenya promises to be an unforgettable part of your safari adventure.

    2. Day 2 Full Day exploration

      On your second day, kick-start your morning with a captivating nature walk and hike around the majestic Mount Kenya, where you'll delve into the extraordinary flora and fauna of the region.

      With a range of trekking options catering to different preferences and energy levels, you'll have the flexibility to choose between leisurely strolls and more demanding hikes. Later in the morning, continue your exploration by venturing into the picturesque foothills of Mount Kenya. After a rewarding morning of discovery, indulge in an afternoon of relaxation at your accommodation or opt for additional activities at your leisure.

    3. Day 3 Mt Kenya To Ol Pejeta

      On your third day, embrace the morning with a captivating nature walk and hike around the majestic Mount Kenya, immersing yourself in the unique flora and fauna of the area. With various trekking options available to suit different preferences and energy levels, you'll have the flexibility to choose from leisurely strolls to more challenging hikes.

      Later in the morning, continue your exploration by venturing into the picturesque foothills of Mount Kenya. After a fulfilling morning of discovery, spend the afternoon at your leisure, whether it's unwinding at your lodging or partaking in optional activities.

    4. Day 4 Ol Pejeta To Masai Mara

      Your day kicks off with breakfast at Ol Pejeta Conservancy before embarking on a mid-morning drive to Masai Mara. Upon arrival in the Mara by afternoon, you'll check-in at your chosen accommodation and savor a delightful lunch.

      Spend the afternoon indulging in a thrilling game drive, where you'll encounter the Mara's diverse wildlife. As the evening sets in, unwind with a picturesque dinner under the stars.

    5. Day 5 Mara To Nairobi

      Following a restful night, prepare for an exhilarating sunrise game drive accompanied by a sumptuous bush breakfast amidst the wilderness. After the memorable experience, you'll check out from your accommodation and commence your journey back to Nairobi.

      Along the way, enjoy a final game drive, soaking in the sights and sounds of the savannah. By late afternoon, you'll arrive in Nairobi, marking the end of your one-night safari adventure. Remember to stay updated with travel advisories for the latest information and ensure a safe and enjoyable journey.

    • Other FAQ's

    Cultural Encounters: Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of the region by visiting local communities and engaging with Maasai tribespeople. Gain insight into their traditional way of life, customs, and rituals, and perhaps participate in cultural activities such as dance performances or beadwork demonstrations.

    Expert Guided Tours: Benefit from the expertise of experienced safari guides who provide insightful commentary, share fascinating wildlife facts, and ensure your safety and enjoyment throughout the journey. Their knowledge of the local flora, fauna, and cultural heritage enhances the overall safari experience.

    Itinerary Familiarization: Reviewing the trip itinerary, including daily trekking distances, elevation gains, and accommodation details, to understand what to expect each day.

    Scenic Landscapes and Photography Opportunities: Capture stunning landscapes, dramatic sunsets, and up-close wildlife encounters with your camera as you traverse diverse terrain, from mountainous slopes to sweeping savannahs. The safari offers numerous photo opportunities for photography enthusiasts to document their unforgettable experiences.

    Plains Safari Adventures: Delve into the expansive plains of Kenya's renowned national parks and reserves, such as the Masai Mara, Ol Pejeta Conservancy, or Amboseli National Park. Enjoy thrilling game drives in search of the Big Five and other iconic wildlife species, including lions, elephants, giraffes, and more.

    1. What is the accommodation like during the safari?

    - Optimal Months: Consider trekking during the dry seasons (January to February and July to October) for favorable weather conditions and clear skies.

    - Peak Climbing Season: Plan your visit during the peak climbing season for bustling trails and vibrant wildlife sightings.

    - Off-Peak Benefits: Explore Mount Kenya during the off-peak seasons (March to June and November to December) for quieter trails and potentially lower accommodation rates.

    2. Is transportation provided during the safari?

    Yes, transportation is included, typically in safari vehicles or minibusses, with experienced drivers and guides.

    3. What kind of wildlife can be expected during the plains safari?:

    -Wildlife sightings may include the Big Five (lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhinoceros), as well as a variety of other species such as giraffes, zebras, antelopes, and more.

    4. Are there any physical fitness requirements for the safari?

    -While some activities may involve walking or hiking, there are usually options available for individuals of varying fitness levels.

    5.Are meals included in the safari package?

    -Yes, most safari packages include meals, typically breakfast, lunch, and dinner, with options for special dietary requirements upon request.
