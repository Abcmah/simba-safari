All about Masai Mara, Amboseli,Tsavo,
Best Season: January - February, June - October

Wet Season: March - May, November - December

Suitable for bird watching as migratory birds flock the region

Best Season: Easy to spot animals as they congregate around water sources. Perfect time as well as there is the great Mara migration in Masai Mara

Tsavo East and West National Parks, covering vast expanses, are celebrated for their varied terrain and rich wildlife. Tsavo East, with its expansive savannahs and iconic red soil, is home to large herds of elephants roaming amidst acacia trees. Meanwhile, Tsavo West captivates with its rugged landscapes, featuring the imposing Chyulu Hills and the tranquil Mzima Springs, where hippos and crocodiles dwell.

Nestled at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro, Amboseli National Park offers mesmerizing vistas of Africa's tallest peak alongside its diverse habitats of grasslands and swamps. Here, elephants roam freely, often against the backdrop of the majestic mountain, while lions, cheetahs, and buffaloes also thrive in this unique ecosystem.

Lastly, the Masai Mara National Reserve stands out for its unparalleled wildlife spectacles, notably the annual wildebeest migration. Across the vast Mara plains, visitors can witness the thrilling crossings of the Mara River, where crocodiles lurk beneath the surface, while the Big Five and an array of other species roam freely in this iconic wilderness.

    1. Day 1 mombasa to Tsavo

      Embark on a thrilling journey from Mombasa or Diani to the captivating landscapes of Tsavo East and West National Parks. Starting early at 6:00 am EAT, your professional guide picks you up from your location for an adventure-packed day. Traveling via Emali along the Nairobi-Mombasa Highway, soak in the scenic views and make occasional stops for rest.

      In Tsavo West, marvel at its rugged terrain teeming with diverse wildlife, including kudus, oryx, and the iconic red elephants. Don't miss the chance to visit Mzima Springs for a serene oasis experience. Then, venture into Tsavo East's expansive savannahs and dense thickets, home to majestic elephants, lions, and giraffes.

      After exhilarating safaris, unwind at your lodge or camp, relishing a delicious dinner as you bask in the enchanting ambiance of the African wilderness.

    2. Day 2 Tsavo East To Tsavo West Safai

      Leave Tsavo East and journey to Tsavo West National Park. Upon arrival, settle into your accommodation and begin a game drive.

      Tsavo West is famous for its striking landscapes, including volcanic hills, lava flows, and natural springs. Discover highlights such as Mzima Springs, where you can observe hippos and crocodiles from an underwater viewing chamber.

      As the day draws to a close, enjoy the peaceful atmosphere of the African wilderness from your lodge or camp in Tsavo West.

    3. Day 3 Amboseli Transfer

      After breakfast, check out by 7:30 am EAT and set off for Amboseli. Along the way, enjoy a game drive to visit the Shetani Lava, formed 500 years ago, known locally as the "devil's lava" due to its fiery emergence. Upon arrival in Amboseli, savor lunch before a brief afternoon rest, followed by an evening game drive.

      Amboseli, named for its "salty dust" in Maasai, boasts stunning views of Mt. Kilimanjaro. It's renowned for its large herds of elephants and diverse habitats, including dried-up lake beds, wetlands, savannas, and woodlands. Explore these landscapes on game drives, spotting elephants, wildebeests, zebras, and more against the backdrop of Kilimanjaro's majestic peak.

      Retreat to your lodge or camp as the day ends, relishing the tranquility of Amboseli's wilderness.

    4. Day 4 Full Day Amboseli

      The following day, start with breakfast and then head out for a full-day game drive to spot wildlife like elephants, lions, and buffalos. Take a break for lunch and a short hike at Amboseli Observation Hill. Return to the lodge for relaxation before enjoying a relaxing sundowner.

      End the day with a delicious dinner and a comfortable night's stay, getting ready for more adventures tomorrow.

    5. Day 5 Masai Mara Transer

      Start your day with a delightful breakfast before saying goodbye to Amboseli and beginning your scenic journey to the iconic Masai Mara National Reserve.

      Keep your eyes peeled for wildlife sightings as you drive through picturesque landscapes. Upon arrival at Masai Mara, check in at your lodge or camp and indulge in a delicious lunch. In the afternoon, embark on your first game drive in this renowned wilderness area, celebrated for its incredible biodiversity and stunning wildlife sightings. Capture memorable moments as you explore the vast plains, home to a variety of predators and prey.

      As the sun sets over the savannah, return to your accommodation for a tranquil evening, reflecting on the day's adventures and eagerly anticipating tomorrow's safari.

    6. Day 6 Mara Excursion

      Start your day with breakfast at 7:00 a.m. EAT, followed by a full-day game drive to spot wildlife and explore the border between Masai Mara National Reserve and Serengeti National Reserve. Enjoy picnic lunches in the wilderness and witness the Great Migration spectacle (seasonal: June to September). After a thrilling day, unwind by the campfire, reflecting on your adventures under the African sky. Then, head back to the lodge for dinner, relaxation, and an overnight stay.

    7. Day 7 Mara To Nairobi

      After enjoying breakfast, check out of the lodge by 8:30 am EAT and visit the Maasai Village for a cultural experience. Then, drive back to Nairobi via the Narok-Mai Mahiu-Nairobi road, making stops for health breaks and shopping for souvenirs or snacks. This concludes your wonderful adventure in Kenya. Bid farewell to the Masai Mara over a final breakfast amidst the African bush's sights and sounds. Following check-out, embark on a scenic journey back to Nairobi, reflecting on the remarkable experiences from your safari.

      Arrive in Nairobi with the option for drop-off at hotels or the airport for your departure, concluding your unforgettable safari journey with cherished memories.

    Lodges
    Voi Safari Lodge

    Voi Safari Lodge, located in Tsavo East National Park, offers stunning views of the park's plains and nearby waterholes, attracting diverse wildlife. Accommodation includes comfortable rooms and cottages designed to blend with the surroundings.

    Guests enjoy game viewing from elevated points, including a rooftop terrace and an underground tunnel to a waterhole, perfect for close-up encounters with elephants and buffaloes. Additional amenities include a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar, providing relaxation after thrilling safari adventures.

    Ngulia Safari Lodge

    Ngulia Safari Lodge is nestled in Tsavo West National Park, offering guests a spectacular vantage point overlooking the vast wilderness. Situated atop the Ngulia Hills, the lodge provides breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and its diverse wildlife. Guests can enjoy comfortable accommodation in well-appointed rooms, each equipped with modern amenities for a relaxing stay.

    Ngulia Safari Lodge is renowned for its unique feature of floodlit waterholes, attracting a variety of wildlife, including elephants, buffaloes, and rhinos, providing an unforgettable safari experience right from the lodge premises. With its prime location and excellent hospitality, Ngulia Safari Lodge promises an unforgettable retreat in the heart of Tsavo West National Park.

    Amboseli Lodge

    Amboseli Lodge is nestled at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro, offering breathtaking views of Africa's highest peak. The lodge features comfortable accommodations, ranging from cozy rooms to luxurious tents, each designed to immerse guests in the surrounding wilderness while providing modern amenities. Guests can indulge in delicious meals served at the on-site restaurant, showcasing a blend of local and international cuisine.

    The lodge also offers various activities, including guided game drives, nature walks, and cultural visits to nearby Maasai villages. With its stunning location and warm hospitality, Amboseli Lodge provides an unforgettable safari experience in the heart of Kenya's Amboseli National Park.

    Mara Sopa lodge

    Mara Sopa Lodge offers a unique safari experience in the heart of the Masai Mara National Reserve. Nestled atop a hill, the lodge provides breathtaking views of the savannah plains and wildlife-rich landscapes. Accommodation options include spacious and comfortable rooms with en-suite bathrooms, designed to blend seamlessly with the natural surroundings.

    Guests can indulge in delicious meals at the on-site restaurant, showcasing a variety of local and international cuisines. The lodge also features a swimming pool, perfect for relaxing after a day of safari adventures. With its prime location and excellent amenities, Mara Sopa Lodge promises an unforgettable stay amidst the beauty of the Masai Mara.
